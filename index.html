The Sales and Install Process

The Sales and Install Process (SIP) is crucial for delivering consistent and successful customer interactions. It helps us understand what good looks like and delivers the best customer experience.

Appointment Readiness

Review Customer Info

Review the customer’s information (name, job notes, etc.). Check for any special circumstances that have been requested from NIS. Reviewing the customer's order and potential add-ons before your visit is crucial. It makes upselling difficult when you don’t have the product with you. Also, this ensures that you have the right tools for the installation, which is a top priority!

Discover Interests

Look for visual cues such as cars, boats, toys, and alarm signs. Understand the neighborhood type, the presence of schools, and any other relevant information. This helps you tailor your approach and connect with the customer on a personal level.

Preempt Potential Issues

Identify and address potential issues before they become problems. This includes ensuring your tools are ready, understanding any safety protocols, and being aware of any competitive products the customer may already have.

Introduction

Win a Friend

Make a great first impression by being friendly, respectful, and engaging. Use last names and proper greetings until rapport is built. Compliment the customer and find common interests to build a connection.

Establish Customer Baseline

Ask questions to understand the customer’s needs and expectations. Find out how they heard about Vivint, what they know about smart home products, and any specific requirements they might have.

Outline Appointment

Verify the customer’s information, tour the home, and discuss the work order. Reinforce that you are the expert and will take care of everything they might need. Plant seeds for future steps in the process.

Walkthrough/Design

Preempt Objections

Address potential objections before the customer brings them up. This keeps the momentum moving forward and shows that you are in control of the process.

Customize/Build Value

Customize the installation to meet the customer’s specific needs. Explain the benefits of each product and how it fits into their lifestyle. Use personal stories and examples to build value.

Verify Understanding

Ensure the customer understands the value of the products and the installation process. Listen carefully to their feedback and clarify any misunderstandings.

Close

Solution/Cost Review

Review the proposed solution and its cost. Explain financing options and the benefits of the products. Ensure the customer understands the value they are getting.

Overcome Objections

Address any objections the customer may have. Use techniques such as "feel, felt, found" to empathize with the customer and provide solutions.

Start Transaction

Begin the transaction process by collecting necessary information and setting expectations for the installation.

Set Work Expectations

Clearly communicate the timeline and expectations for the installation. Under-promise and over-deliver to ensure customer satisfaction.

Outline Benefits

Reinforce the benefits of the products and services. Use this opportunity to plant seeds for future upsells and referrals.

Review

Review Completed Work

Ensure that all work is completed to a high standard. Check that all products are installed correctly and that the customer is satisfied with the result.

Educate Customer

Take the time to educate the customer on how to use their new system. Ensure they are comfortable and confident in using the products.

Benefits Follow-Up

Follow up with the customer to reinforce the benefits of their new system. Use this opportunity to discuss additional products and services that may benefit them.
